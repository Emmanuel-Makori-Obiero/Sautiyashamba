🌱 The Problem

Mama Njeri Didn't Fail.
The System Failed Her.

Mama Njeri is sixty-two years old, and her hands are the only insurance policy her family has. In the misty highlands of Karatina, her small plot of kale is more than food — it is her grandson's school fees and her own retirement.

Three weeks ago, a strange yellow fungus began to breathe across her leaves. Her son sent her a YouTube link. But the screen stayed black — the data was off. The link was a door she didn't have the key to.

Out of desperation, she walked to the village center and spent her last savings on a bottle of pesticide she couldn't read, sold by someone who didn't care. The chemical was toxic. By morning, her entire harvest was scorched — blackened. Forty years of farming wisdom, defeated by a single link she couldn't open.

💡 Mama Njeri's story is not exceptional — it is the statistical norm for 4.5 million Kenyan farmers. Sauti ya Shamba is the system that should have been there.
⚙️ The Solution

How Sauti ya Shamba Works

A "Reverse Innovation" approach: cutting-edge Generative AI powering legacy telecommunications. No internet. No smartphone. No cost to the farmer.

01
📞

Zero-Data Access

Farmers call a Toll-Free IVR number or dial a USSD code on any basic feature phone. No data bundles. No internet. Works on a KES 500 "kabambe."

02
🎤

Voice in Local Language

The AI greets farmers in Gikuyu or Swahili and understands colloquial descriptions — including local names like "mdudu wa fedha" (silver bug = Spider Mites).

03
🧠

RAG-Verified AI Advice

Google Gemini retrieves answers exclusively from KALRO/FAO-verified manuals. A content filter blocks all banned chemicals before the response is spoken.

04
🔊

Warm, Localised Voice

Answers are spoken back in a voice cloned from a real local agronomist — not a robot. 89% of test farmers rated it "friendly and trustworthy."

05
🔁

Human Escalation

High-risk chemical queries are flagged to a live agronomist dashboard. If the AI solution fails, a human callback is triggered automatically.

06
📊

Outbreak Intelligence

Every call contributes to a real-time pest-outbreak map sold to county governments and NGOs — funding the free service for farmers.

📰 Kenya Market Evidence

This Is Not Hypothetical.
Every Number Is Verified.

Research from the Daily Nation, The Standard, KIPPRA, WRI Africa, and global databases confirms the exact crisis Sauti ya Shamba addresses.

🔴 Digital Divide
59.2%

Of Kenyans Were Offline in 2024

32.93 million people had no internet access. 70.2% of Kenyans live in rural areas where connectivity is lowest. Only 20–30% of farmers adopt digital agri-tech despite 95 platforms existing.

→ DataReportal, Feb 2024 & KIPPRA Feb 2024
🔴 Pesticide Crisis
100%

Of 70 Farmers Tested Positive for Banned Paraquat

A January 2026 Daily Nation investigation in Meru County found the landscape "dotted with fresh graves of farmers who succumbed to cancer." Paraquat was banned in the UK in 2007 — but is still sold freely in Kenyan agro-dealer shops.

→ Daily Nation, Jan 13, 2026
🟡 Counterfeit Inputs
KES 120B

Lost Annually to Counterfeit Agro-Chemicals

89.16% of surveyed consumers identify pesticides as Kenya's most counterfeited input. 60.48% of fake agrochemicals are sold through informal kiosks — exactly where Mama Njeri shops.

→ ACA National Survey 2025 & ACA
🟡 Wrong Diagnosis
150

KALRO Plant Health Experts — For All of Kenya

KALRO confirmed (Daily Nation, Jan 2026) that farmers routinely buy chemicals with no diagnosis. 150 experts were deployed — covering millions of farms. Government budget cuts have made extension officer visits "rarer."

→ Daily Nation, Jan 17, 2026 & Mozilla Foundation 2024
🔴 Food Loss Emergency
KES 72B

Annual Food Loss — While 15M Kenyans Go Hungry

Kenya wastes 30–40% of all food produced annually. The Daily Nation (Sept 2025) reported 20–36% of the maize harvest is lost in storage — while 15 million citizens (28%) face daily food insecurity.

→ Daily Nation, Sept 10, 2025 & WRI Africa 2025
🟢 Policy Mandate
2024

Parliament Confirmed Extension Services Are Broken

Parliament's Agriculture Extension Bill 2024 acknowledged that most counties have no extension policy at all. The AIRC was described as "poorly resourced." Sauti ya Shamba is filling the institutional void.

→ Daily Nation, Oct 14, 2025 & The Standard, March 2026
